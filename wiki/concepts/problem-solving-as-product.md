# Problem-Solving as Product

Problem-solving as product is the claim that the unit of software is being redefined: not a downloadable app full of tools the user operates, but a *workflow* or *task-completion service* driven by natural language, whose actual offering is "problem-solving itself." Where a traditional app hands the user features and lets them assemble the solution, an AI-era product *delivers the solved problem* and abstracts the tooling away behind an interface. The corollary is a new front door — an "AI surface" — and a new competitive logic, in which a focused vertical solution grounded in proprietary data beats a generalist that can technically do anything.

## Key Mechanics

- **The offering is the solution, not the tool**: the product's value is the completed outcome, so the user states a goal in natural language and receives the result rather than navigating menus and features — the consumer-facing endpoint of `[[software-3-0]]`, where a prompt yields the work.
- **App → workflow / task-completion service**: applications stop being installable bundles and become services that run a workflow to completion, shifting the design question from "what features" to "what problem does this finish."
- **The "AI surface" as the new UI**: the interface generalizes beyond a screen — conversational UI today, voice next, and potentially integrated neural interfaces (Neuralink) later — so the surface through which a human invokes problem-solving is itself an evolving design axis, the delivery-side cousin of `[[interactive-artifacts]]`.
- **Generalist vs. vertical tension as the strategic fork**: a generalist system (the source cites Codex aiming to do *any* task) competes against vertical solutions that win by leaning on proprietary data and specialized tools — "unbundling ChatGPT" by carving a niche the generalist serves only shallowly, the same expert-domain logic of `[[expert-domain-defensibility]]`.
- **Value migrates from creation to brand and distribution**: as general software creation is commoditized, the market rewards distribution and brand and the ability to be the "only one" credible offering for a specific problem — so the durable move is to identify a *real customer problem* and ship a *complete* solution rather than a general-purpose tool.

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- **It is the product-shape of `[[expert-domain-defensibility]]`**: that concept maps *where* the defensible region is (the expert domain, proprietary data, vertical workflows); this names *what the product becomes* there — a complete solution that finishes a problem, not a tool layered on a frontier model. They compose, and both reject the thin-app-on-a-generalist play.
- **Complete solution vs. distribution-first**: delivering "problem-solving itself" demands depth (proprietary data, end-to-end workflow), pulling against `[[distribution-over-features]]`'s "ship one feature, market hard" bet — depth-of-solution and breadth-of-reach are different routes to value, reconciled only when a focused solution is also easy to distribute.
- **Proprietary context is the moat**: a vertical solution wins on data and specialized tooling the generalist lacks — the `[[second-brain]]` thesis that injected unique context, not model choice, is the differentiator, applied to a product rather than a person.
- **The AI surface is speculative beyond text/voice**: conversational and voice surfaces exist; neural interfaces are a forecast, so the durable claim is *the surface is an evolving design axis*, not any specific endpoint.
- **Vantage caveat**: the framing is a forward-looking thesis from a discussion podcast, so the "apps are dead, workflows win" claim is directional rather than measured — the enduring idea is product-as-solved-problem, not the timeline.
