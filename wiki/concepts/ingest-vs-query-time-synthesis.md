# Ingest-Time vs. Query-Time Synthesis

Ingest-time vs. query-time synthesis is the architectural choice of *when* an AI knowledge system does its hard thinking — reconciling contradictions, building cross-references, and distilling understanding — at the moment information is **ingested**, or at the moment a question is **asked**. An ingest-time system (Karpathy's AI Wiki) compiles understanding once as sources arrive, writing browsable wiki pages that are cheap to query later but risk drifting from the raw source. A query-time system (Open Brain) stores raw facts faithfully and synthesizes fresh on every question, preserving data integrity and multi-agent scalability at the cost of re-deriving understanding each time. The two are not rival tools but opposite placements of the same synthesis cost, and the corpus's resolution is a hybrid that combines them.

## Key Mechanics

- **Ingest-time synthesis (the wiki)**: the AI does the heavy lifting when new information *comes in* — reading, synthesizing, updating existing pages, flagging contradictions, and building cross-references at ingestion, so knowledge is "compiled once and then kept current." The payoff is pre-built, browsable understanding that is cheap to query and an evolving personal model of a domain, like a tutor continuously updating a study guide as new material is covered.
- **Query-time synthesis (the store)**: the AI stores raw information faithfully — tagging and categorizing efficiently *at input* — and defers the hard thinking to *query time*, reading the relevant data on demand and producing a fresh, precise synthesis. Like a librarian with a perfectly organized filing cabinet generating exact answers on the fly, it preserves raw facts and unresolved contradictions without forcing them into a single narrative.
- **The RAG-restarts-from-scratch baseline**: a pure query-time retrieval system *without accumulation* — ordinary RAG — re-derives an answer from the raw documents on *every* query, building on nothing from prior interactions. This is the failure mode ingest-time synthesis is designed to escape: the wiki accumulates pre-built connections and synthesis so a later, similar, or cross-document question is answered from an organized base rather than from zero each time. Query-time synthesis *with* a faithful structured store sits between the two — it re-derives per query but over an accumulated, organized corpus rather than re-scanning raw documents blind.
- **Oracle → Maintainer role shift**: ingest-time synthesis reframes the AI from a transient "Oracle" answering one-off questions into a persistent "Maintainer" performing an ongoing job of building and improving a knowledge artifact over time — the same accumulation discipline as `[[compound-engineering]]`, here applied to a knowledge base rather than a codebase.
- **The hybrid (the corpus's recommendation)**: use a query-time structured store as the permanent, high-volume, authoritative "durable memory layer" and the single source of truth; layer an ingest-time view *on top* by running a scheduled compilation agent that reads the structured data (an "Open Brain graph"), synthesizes it, and emits dynamic wiki pages or topic summaries. This yields a browsable, evolving artifact whose every claim is grounded in and regenerable from the authoritative data.
- **Error correction by regeneration**: because the database stays the source of truth, the hybrid prevents error *compounding* — any mistake in a generated wiki page is fixed by correcting the source data and rebuilding the view, rather than by editing a synthesized summary that has already drifted.
- **Timing-independent principles**: both paradigms share user ownership ("file over app" / no-SaaS middlemen), human curation of inputs, intentional structure over random accumulation, and agent-first design (the AI is the primary reader; human readability is a beneficial byproduct).

## How It Appears in the Corpus

The Nate B Jones ("AI News & Strategy Daily") analysis builds the entire comparison: Karpathy's AI Wiki is cast as the ingest-time system (synthesis at the point information arrives, producing a browsable Obsidian-style artifact), Open Brain (`[[cross-tool-memory]]`) as the query-time system (faithful raw storage, synthesis on demand), and a hybrid as the most robust answer — Open Brain's structured store as the authoritative core with a scheduled compilation agent generating a wiki layer over it. The video names the AI's "Oracle → Maintainer" role shift as the deeper insight and warns that an unmaintained ingest-time wiki can become *active misinformation*.

The Teacher's Tech "Karpathy's LLM Wiki — Full Beginner Setup Guide" tutorial frames the same distinction from the beginner side by contrasting ingest-time synthesis with the *no-accumulation* query-time baseline: it diagnoses ordinary RAG as approaching every query "from scratch" — retrieving and generating an answer each time with no memory and no building upon prior interactions — and presents Karpathy's LLM Wiki as the fix, where the AI reads sources *once* at ingestion to build a persistent, interlinked wiki (extracting key ideas, updating existing pages, creating and linking new concepts, flagging contradictions) so knowledge compounds and later questions are answered from an organized, accumulated base. It is the clearest statement in the corpus of *why* one would pay the ingest-time cost: to escape the repetitive, non-compounding work of query-from-scratch retrieval.

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- **Active misinformation is the ingest-time hazard**: because an ingest-time wiki *synthesizes* rather than stores, a stale page does not merely go missing — its confident prose can silently assert a synthesis that has drifted from the raw source, a sharper failure than the freshness drift of `[[llm-knowledge-wiki]]` or the dilution of `[[context-rot]]` because the wrong answer is *trusted*. The editorial decisions the AI makes while synthesizing can also obscure nuance present in the raw material. Query-time synthesis avoids this by re-deriving from faithful raw data each time, which is precisely why the hybrid keeps the structured store authoritative.
- **Scalability vs. pre-built depth**: ingest-time wikis scale poorly to teams and multiple agents (merge conflicts, a single narrative focus) but deliver pre-built understanding and connections; query-time stores scale to structured queries, high volume, and multi-agent access but historically offered less pre-built synthesis depth, paying re-derivation cost on every query — the very `grep`-style inefficiency Karpathy's wiki was meant to cure (`[[llm-knowledge-wiki]]`).
- **No-accumulation is the worst of query-time**: a pure RAG system that re-scans raw documents per query (no organized store, no compounding) pays the re-derivation cost *and* keeps no benefit between queries — the explicit baseline the ingest-time wiki improves on. Query-time synthesis is only competitive when it runs over a faithful *accumulated* store, not over raw documents from scratch.
- **Compute now or compute later**: the choice is essentially *store the synthesis* (ingest-time, risking staleness) versus *recompute it on demand* (query-time, paying per query) — a knowledge-management echo of the store-vs-recompute decision the corpus draws in serving systems, with freshness rather than memory bandwidth as the resource being traded.
- **The hybrid is the most infrastructure**: a structured store plus a scheduled compilation agent is heavier to stand up than either standalone approach, and is itself the kind of always-on background job described in `[[agent-as-infrastructure]]`; its single-source-of-truth discipline is what converts that cost into error-resistance rather than just more moving parts.
