# Internalized Orchestration

Internalized orchestration is the technique of taking the *orchestration scaffold* that usually lives in an external agent harness — sampling many candidate solutions in parallel, then cross-verifying them into one answer — and training it directly into the model's parameters as an activatable "inner skill," so the model performs deep, multi-path reasoning natively without a brittle external system driving it. Its motivating diagnosis is the *agentic harness bottleneck*: the intricate, often unstable external frameworks that orchestrate test-time scaling obscure (and cap) the intrinsic reasoning a model could do, so the scaffolding meant to elicit capability becomes the thing limiting it. The fix is to strip the orchestration out of the harness and fold it into the weights, making high-level reasoning portable across environments instead of bound to one fragile orchestrator. It is the corpus's sharpest evidence that orchestration logic itself can migrate into the model — the parameter-level endpoint of the absorption dynamic that `[[super-app-convergence]]` describes at the product level.

## Key Mechanics

- **Two-stage native process — parallel reasoning then sequential deliberation**: the internalized skill runs in two phases. First, *K* independent reasoning agents spawn trajectories simultaneously with no cross-talk, generating diverse initial solutions — the breadth pass. Then a *serialized memory cache* compresses all those randomized trajectories into a single prompt context, and a *sequential deliberation* phase forces the model to cross-verify logic *across* the paths rather than merely stitching fragments together — the depth pass. It is the parallel diversity of `[[cross-model-critique]]` and `[[agent-swarm-orchestration]]` plus the checking step of `[[generator-verifier-loop]]`, both collapsed into one model's forward behavior.
- **Deliberation as a true verifier**: the cross-verification phase functions as a genuine verifier, not a vote — it can *rectify* errors even when the initial parallel pass rate is below 0.5, breaking through the hard statistical ceiling that simple majority voting (`vote@k`) cannot cross. The corpus reports `heavy mean@k` and `heavy pass@k` consistently dominating `mean@k`/`vote@k` baselines, with `heavy mean@k` reaching 100% at K=16 on a STEM benchmark and ~90% accuracy (vs. an 83.3% voting baseline) when paired with a Python interpreter — the deliberation supplying the constructible verifier that `[[verifiability-law]]` says autonomy depends on.
- **A readable skill document, not code**: the whole protocol is specified in a text-based "skill document" — activation conditions, parallel-reasoning protocol, deliberation prompt, and strict output constraints — that any LLM orchestrator can execute to trigger deep reasoning *without code changes*. This is the `[[thin-harness-fat-skills]]` idea taken to its limit: the orchestration is a prose skill the model runs, and the heavy thinking is parameterized behind it.
- **An optimal deliberation cutoff**: extending the recursive deliberation loop refines collective reasoning (raising `heavy mean@k`) but can *lower* the maximum potential (`heavy pass@k`) as cumulative noise and bias accumulate — so there is an optimal stopping point for information consistency, the deliberation analogue of the runaway-loop and safety-cap concerns in `[[generator-verifier-loop]]`.
- **Consensus-grounded input selection ("max answer num")**: when choosing which parallel trajectories to load into the memory cache, prioritizing the ones yielding the *most frequent* answers ("max answer num") beats "max length" (which injects noise) and "max diversity" (which degrades to random sampling) — the deliberation needs a reliable, consensus-based foundation as its input rather than maximal variety.
- **Scaled by RLVR at the parameter level**: Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (`[[reasoning-reinforcement-learning]]`-style) optimizes the skill's parameters, simultaneously growing the *breadth* of parallel generation and the *depth* of critical deliberation — genuine test-time scaling moved into the weights rather than bolted on as external orchestration.

## How It Appears in the Corpus

The "Research Paper Review" video on HEAVYSKILL presents "heavy skill" as a methodology that internalizes agentic orchestration into an LLM as a parameterized inner skill to overcome the "agentic harness bottleneck." It details the two-stage architecture (K parallel agents with no cross-talk → a serialized memory cache → sequential cross-verifying deliberation), defines the protocol in a code-free skill document, and reports STEM results where `heavy mean@k`/`heavy pass@k` dominate `mean@k`/`vote@k` (100% `heavy mean@k` at K=16 on AMIE 25 with DeepSeek V3.2; ~90% with a Python interpreter vs. an 83.3% vote baseline), with deliberation rectifying errors even below a 0.5 parallel pass rate. It surfaces the optimal-deliberation-cutoff trade-off, the "max answer num" trajectory-selection finding, and RLVR as the optimizer that scales parallel breadth and deliberative depth together — concluding that decoupling high-level reasoning from brittle external infrastructure maximizes portability and could render today's heavy external orchestration frameworks obsolete.

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- **The parameter-level case for `[[super-app-convergence]]`**: where super-app convergence observes harness *features* being absorbed into models at the product level, internalized orchestration is the same absorption applied to *orchestration logic itself*, trained into the weights — direct evidence for the `[[harness-engineering]]` forecast that harnesses simplify as models improve, and in tension with the elaborate multi-workflow `[[deterministic-workflow-orchestration]]` direction whose value erodes if the model internalizes the coordination.
- **It does not abolish the verifier dependency, it relocates it**: the deliberation phase works because it acts as a real verifier, so the result is bounded by how cleanly the task can be scored — the `[[verifiability-law]]` precondition. On unverifiable tasks the same cross-checking can converge confidently on a wrong consensus, and the "max answer num" reliance on frequency is itself a `[[reward-hacking]]`-adjacent risk: the most common answer is not guaranteed to be the correct one.
- **Internalized verification can still share one model's blind spots**: collapsing parallel generation and deliberation into a single model removes the orchestration brittleness but also removes the *model diversity* that `[[cross-model-critique]]` and `[[holdout-validation]]` rely on — K trajectories from one model can co-fail in correlated ways that no amount of internal cross-verification surfaces.
- **More deliberation is not more correctness**: the documented cutoff where extending the loop *lowers* peak potential is the internalized counterpart to `[[tokens-to-think]]`'s caveat — extra reasoning raises the chance of a right answer but accrues noise past an optimal point, so the depth pass needs a stopping rule rather than unbounded recursion.
- **Portability vs. the cost of the breadth-and-depth pass**: running K parallel trajectories and a serialized deliberation over all of them is token-heavy by construction (`[[token-maxing]]` internalized), trading the external harness's coordination cost for inference cost concentrated in one model — cheaper to deploy across environments, but not cheap to run.
- **Vantage caveat**: the framing and figures come from a single research-paper-review summary of one method, so the specific benchmark numbers (100% at K=16, 90% with tools) and the "renders external frameworks obsolete" claim are illustrative of the *pattern* — orchestration trained into the model as an activatable inner skill — rather than a settled or independently reproduced result.
