# Distribution Over Features

Distribution over features is the strategy that, in a world where building an AI app is cheap, the binding constraint on success is *distribution*, not capability — so the winning move is to ship a single high-value feature and spend the overwhelming majority of effort on marketing it. It names two common failure modes and inverts them: builders overcomplicate the first version (when one focused feature would be enough) and neglect marketing (when marketing is where the leverage actually is). Simplicity is not a compromise but a distribution advantage: a one-line product is easy to describe, easy to share by word of mouth, and easy for influencers to demonstrate.

## Key Mechanics

- **One feature that delivers immediate value**: the first version does exactly one thing well (the source's example is a calorie tracker that reads a photo), giving a user an obvious payoff on first use rather than a broad, shallow tool.
- **Simplicity as a sharing primitive**: a narrow product is trivial to describe in a sentence, which is what makes word-of-mouth and influencer content possible — complexity is friction on every distribution channel at once.
- **90/10 effort split toward marketing**: after launch, roughly 90% of effort goes to marketing rather than feature work, treating the build as nearly done and distribution as the real job.
- **Find a repeatable viral format**: the goal is to discover a content format that reliably earns views and then reuse it deliberately — either by producing your own social content in proven viral structures or by paying micro-influencers who consistently hit high view counts. Reach is engineered, not hoped for.

## How It Appears in the Corpus

The Sabrina Ramonov "5 Ways to Make Money in 2026 (with AI)" tutorial frames "vibe code a simple AI app and prioritize marketing" around exactly this inversion: avoid overcomplicating v1 and avoid neglecting marketing, start with one immediately-valuable feature, then spend 90% of effort on distribution — making your own content in viral formats or paying consistent micro-influencers — with the explicit aim of finding and repeatedly reusing a "repeatable viral format." The same viral-format-replication advice anchors the video's personal-brand path (copy proven viral content, then develop a style).

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- **Cheap to build is the precondition, not the win**: the strategy only makes sense because `[[vibe-coding-vs-agentic-engineering|vibe coding]]` has collapsed build cost — but a frictionless build raises the floor for everyone, so distribution, not the feature, becomes the scarce resource and the actual differentiator.
- **Narrow feature vs. defensibility**: a single easily-copied feature is easy to share *and* easy to clone, so distribution speed must outrun imitation — the marketing engine, not the feature, has to be the moat, which is a harder thing to sustain.
- **Pairs with the wedge, but inverts its depth**: it shares the "win one thing" instinct of `[[founder-wedge-strategy]]`, yet a wedge wins by *deep* embedding in a workflow while distribution-over-features wins by *broad, fast* reach — opposite bets on where defensibility comes from.
- **A repeatable viral format is itself perishable**: formats saturate and platforms change, so "find the format" is a recurring search, not a one-time discovery; the operationalization of converting that reach into revenue is taken up by `[[ai-marketing-automation]]`.
- **Tutorial-vantage claims**: the 90/10 split and the primacy of marketing are asserted heuristics from a monetization video, not measured results, and assume a consumer product where word-of-mouth dominates.
