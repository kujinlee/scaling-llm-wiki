## 1. Agentic AI: Beyond Chatbots and RAG
Agentic AI fundamentally shifts the paradigm from simple question-answering, as seen in traditional chatbots and RAG applications, to reliably executing complex, multi-step workflows. Unlike systems that respond to a single prompt, agentic AI can reason, define a plan, utilize various tools (like databases or code interpreters), and work autonomously for extended periods to achieve a given goal. This capability transforms AI from an entertainment or information retrieval tool into a powerful engine for achieving actions and tangible outcomes.

---

## 2. Bridging the Gap from Demo to Production
Many enterprises face significant challenges when moving agentic AI from proof-of-concept demos to production-ready deployments, primarily around data connection, security, cost, and performance. The key differentiator for production readiness lies in control and enforcement. NVIDIA's approach, incorporating a secure runtime like Open Shell, ensures agents adhere to defined guardrails, dictating what systems they can access, what actions they can perform, and where inference occurs. This secure runtime is crucial for enterprises to manage risks and integrate agents into their existing, complex environments.

---

## 3. Rethinking Costs and Unlocking New Markets
While the cost per token for large language models is decreasing, the increased consumption due to agentic AI's expanded capabilities means total costs can still be significant. Enterprises often underestimate the overall cost of a completed task. The true value of agentic AI extends beyond token economics, driving productivity, enabling the creation of new products, and unlocking previously inaccessible markets. Therefore, metrics like "cost per complete task" or "cost per successful workflow" become more relevant than just token cost, reflecting the broader business impact.

---

## 4. The Power of Open Models and Hybrid Architectures
Enterprise AI deployments increasingly leverage a "system of models" rather than relying solely on single cloud APIs or entirely open-source solutions. This often involves combining powerful cloud frontier models for high-level orchestration or planning with specialized, post-trained open models (like NVIDIA's NeMo models) for specific, domain-centric tasks such as research or specialized coding. Open models offer benefits like greater control, customizability through post-training on proprietary data, transparency for trust, and flexibility in deployment environments.

----- 

## 5. Agentic AI in Specialized Engineering Workflows
In highly specialized domains, such as chip design verification, agentic AI acts as more than a co-pilot; it becomes an autonomous engineer. Collaborations, like that between NVIDIA and Cadence, demonstrate agents performing complex, iterative tasks significantly faster (e.g., 40 times faster). These agents can execute multiple iterations towards an objective with minimal human intervention, enabling engineers to focus on higher-level architectural challenges and problem-solving. This capability not only boosts productivity but also allows for an unprecedented scale of design and development.

---

## 6. Strategic Enterprise Adoption
For enterprises adopting agentic AI, the recommended starting point is not a "nice-to-have" feature, but a critical, well-defined business function. Forming a "tiger team" to focus on a specific, important discipline (e.g., software development or chip design) allows for a deep dive into implementation, including observability, policy setting via secure runtimes, and trace collection for continuous improvement. This approach minimizes the risk of starting too broadly while ensuring real value creation, setting a foundation for scaling agentic AI across other parts of the organization.

---

## Conclusion
Agentic AI represents a transformative shift in how AI is utilized within enterprises, moving from reactive responses to proactive, autonomous task execution. While deployment presents challenges related to security, cost, and integration, solutions like secure runtimes and hybrid model strategies are paving the way for production readiness. The ultimate benefit lies in unlocking new market opportunities, revolutionizing specialized workflows, and enhancing overall enterprise productivity, demanding a strategic, focused approach to adoption that prioritizes critical business functions and robust deployment frameworks.
