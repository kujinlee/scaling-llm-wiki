## 1. Building a Self-Improving AI System
▶ [0:08–4:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=8s)
Most AI users build static systems that require constant feeding to improve. This video introduces a method to create a self-improving knowledge base, similar to Andre Karpathy's "LLM wiki" or an "AI map." This system proactively gets smarter daily by forming connections between scattered ideas, leading to breakthroughs. It utilizes existing tools like Claude Co-work or OpenAI's Codeex and a simple file system, allowing for quick setup of an intelligent, self-maintaining agent.

---

## 2. Setting Up Your Knowledge Vault with Obsidian
▶ [4:52–6:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=292s)
To begin, download Obsidian, a free application used to visualize and manage your knowledge base. Create a new "vault," which is essentially a folder on your computer, and name it (e.g., "knowledge base"). Populate this vault by dragging in existing notes or creating new ones directly in Obsidian. To easily add new content from the web, use the Obsidian Web Clipper Chrome extension to save articles or YouTube video transcripts directly to your vault, preparing them for AI processing.

---

## 3. Integrating AI Tools and Expanding Data Input
▶ [6:29–9:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=389s)
Choose an AI tool like Claude Co-work to manage your knowledge base. Point Claude to your Obsidian vault folder, granting it access to all your files. Claude also offers native connectors to various third-party tools like email, calendar, and Notion, integrating them into your knowledge base. For apps without native connectors, utilize Zapier's MCP (Multi-Channel Platform) to link Claude Co-work to thousands of additional applications, allowing the AI to pull information from diverse sources, such as your YouTube account, automatically.

---

## 4. Automating Knowledge Organization and Indexing
▶ [9:29–11:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=569s)
To enable your AI to self-improve, use a specific prompt provided in the video's description. Paste this prompt into Claude Co-work, pointing it to your knowledge base folder. The AI will then organize your knowledge into folders, build an index (or map) of connections, and ask for your approval on its proposed plan. Finally, schedule a daily task (e.g., 2 AM) for the AI to automatically check for new additions to your folder and integrate them into the map, ensuring continuous self-improvement.

---

## 5. Harnessing Your Enriched AI for Advanced Insights
▶ [11:27–13:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=687s)
Once set up, your AI will naturally leverage the knowledge base to provide more relevant and helpful answers without special commands. It can identify patterns, recognize similar past problems, and suggest solutions. You can also explicitly prompt it to find patterns in your thinking, missed opportunities, or potential revenue streams. The video demonstrates this with a business journal, where the AI connects current thoughts to past entries, and a health knowledge base that tracks data for personalized insights.

## Conclusion
▶ [13:32–14:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-QDPnEIkvw&t=812s)
Building a self-building and self-maintaining AI knowledge base is a highly useful system that takes less than an hour to set up. By feeding your AI assistant with organized knowledge, it becomes significantly more powerful, capable of providing deeper insights and more effective assistance across various aspects of your life, from business to personal health.
