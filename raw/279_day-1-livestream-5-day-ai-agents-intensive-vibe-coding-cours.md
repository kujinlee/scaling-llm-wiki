## 2. The New SDLC and Agentic Engineering
▶ [3:26–6:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iic3Zj427M&t=206s)
The white paper for day one emphasizes a profound shift in computing history from translating syntax to expressing intent via natural language, termed "vibe coding." This spectrum ranges from casual prompting to disciplined agentic engineering, where AI operates within structured, deterministic boundaries. Context engineering is identified as the real modern skill, differentiating between static (system instructions) and dynamic (agent skills) contexts. The new Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) collapses implementation time from weeks to minutes, making requirements specification and verification the new human bottlenecks. The formula `Agent = Model + Harness` is introduced, with the harness (sandboxes, tools, orchestration, guardrails) being 90% of a reliable agentic system.

## 3. Impact on CS Education, Hiring, and AI Tools
▶ [6:53–12:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iic3Zj427M&t=413s)
As development shifts to asynchronous orchestration and AI-driven coding, computer science education is expected to evolve, prioritizing high-level architectural judgment over syntactic mastery. The focus will be on *how to think* rather than *how to type*. This change could enable students to build entire systems or businesses during their education. Tools like Google AI Studio are designed to facilitate this, moving from "prompt to prototype" and "prompt to production" towards "prompt to profitable company," empowering individuals to build software businesses much like YouTube empowered creators.

## 4. Advanced AI Agent Capabilities and Architectural Patterns
▶ [12:21–21:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iic3Zj427M&t=741s)
Self-evolving AI agents like DeepMind's Alpha Evolve are transforming industries by optimizing algorithms for complex problems, such as matrix multiplication, large-scale schedulers, and hardware design. These agents act as expert optimizers, enhancing performance across science, cloud, and infrastructure. To address the final 20% milestone of complex tasks with minimal human intervention, successful architectural patterns include agents writing code within sandbox environments, creating their own tools and sub-agents, and robust automated and human-in-the-loop verification loops that generate data for self-improvement over time. The Open Knowledge Format combined with Graph RAG architecture can provide agents with a semantic graph-level understanding of entire systems before writing code, addressing contextual problems in large codebases.

## 5. Risks and Use Cases for Long-Running Agents
▶ [21:05–40:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iic3Zj427M&t=1265s)
The transition to an AI-driven SDLC presents long-term risks, including the erosion of human expertise with codebases, challenges in accountability for issues, and potential lost opportunities for human-driven innovation. Mitigating these requires careful planning to ensure continuous human understanding and oversight, especially concerning security. Long-running autonomous agents are particularly effective for use cases requiring extended operation, such as deep research (collating comprehensive information), AI coding (continuous testing and verification), and dynamic environments where inputs change over time (e.g., loan processing, insurance claims, legal cases). Challenges for long-running agents include optimizing external tools not designed for AI interaction and navigating the "impressive, useful, sustainable" journey of AI application.
