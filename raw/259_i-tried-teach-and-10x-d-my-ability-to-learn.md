## 1. Introducing the /teach AI Skill for Personalized Tutoring
▶ [0:00–0:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=0s)
The video introduces Matt Pocock's free `/teach` skill for Claude Code Agent, transforming it into a stateful personal tutor. Unlike standard AI interactions that forget context, `/teach` remembers user progress, questions, and areas of difficulty, enabling a continuous and personalized learning journey. The speaker highlights it as potentially the most useful skill installed this year for learning diverse topics like coding, frameworks, or open-source models.

---

## 2. The Stateful Advantage Over Traditional AI Tutors
▶ [0:45–2:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=45s)
A key differentiator of `/teach` is its stateful nature. While typical AI chats provide one-off answers and reset context with each new session (likened to a substitute teacher), `/teach` acts as a consistent teacher. It tracks what you've learned, where you're stuck, and logs questions, allowing it to pick the next lesson purposefully based on your current understanding. This ensures continuous progress without repeatedly explaining yourself.

---

## 3. Comprehensive Learning Ecosystem and Interactive Lessons
▶ [2:05–6:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=125s)
The skill creates a structured learning environment within your operating system, including dedicated folders for each topic. This workspace contains learning records, dynamically generated lesson plans (often as interactive HTML files), a growing glossary of terms, a mission statement outlining learning goals, and a curated list of resources. Lessons are action-oriented, featuring install commands, checklists, self-checks, and the ability to generate quizzes, promoting active learning. The AI also adapts lessons to your hardware capabilities, recommending appropriate models.

----- 

## 4. The Pedagogical Foundation: Knowledge, Skills, Wisdom & ZPD
▶ [6:44–8:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=404s)
`/teach` is built upon Matt Pocock's 10 years of teaching experience, focusing on genuine learning rather than just information delivery. Its philosophy covers three levels: acquiring **Knowledge** from vetted sources, building **Skills** through hands-on practice, and gaining **Wisdom** by directing users to relevant communities for collaborative learning. Crucially, it leverages the "Zone of Proximal Development" (ZPD) by tailoring lessons to be challenging yet achievable, ensuring optimal engagement and progress without being too easy or too difficult.

---

## 5. Installing and Customizing Your /teach Workspace
▶ [8:59–12:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=539s)
To install, users fetch the `/teach` skill from the {slash}skills GitHub repository and instruct Claude Code to install it. Initiating a new learning path with `/teach [topic]` prompts the AI to ask about your "why" (to create a mission statement) and desired "depth" (e.g., hands-on code). The process involves web searches for initial information and setting up the workspace. A common issue of incorrect folder placement is noted, with a simple fix involving instructing the agent to move the created subfolder to the desired learning directory.

---

## Conclusion
▶ [12:19–15:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lw6Ld1ZgpV8&t=739s)
While an incredibly powerful tutor, `/teach` is not a shortcut or magic. It's a tool that facilitates effective learning by providing structured, personalized, and interactive content. Users must still commit to doing the "reps" and actively engaging with the material. The core message remains that AI can outsource research but never true understanding; that still comes from personal effort and engagement, with `/teach` serving as an excellent guide until you're ready to join a community.
