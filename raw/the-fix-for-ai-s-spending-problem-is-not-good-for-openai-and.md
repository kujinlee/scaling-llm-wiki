# The Fix For AI's Spending Problem Is Not Good For OpenAI And Anthropic

**Channel:** CNBC | **Duration:** 46:37 | **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w1yB7ck36JA

> [!summary] Quick Reference
> **TL;DR:** This video explores how escalating AI costs are driving enterprises to adopt model routing and decentralized AI, optimizing efficiency and ensuring value.
>
> **Key Takeaways:**
> - Implement model routing to match AI tasks with appropriate models for cost efficiency.
> - Prioritize measuring AI's ROI by engineering output, not just token usage.
> - Prepare for increased network demands due to distributed AI workloads.
> - Explore desk-side computing and smaller, specialized models for local processing.
> - Establish human-in-the-loop processes to build trust in AI agent delegation.
>

## 1. The Costly Reality of Enterprise AI
Corporate America is grappling with the skyrocketing costs of AI, often referred to as “tokenomics.” Many companies, including large enterprises, are quickly exhausting their annual AI budgets due to the prevailing approach of using the most powerful, and expensive, frontier models for all tasks, regardless of complexity. This “old way” is likened to assigning the smartest, highest-paid engineer to every job, from complex problem-solving to simple password resets.

---

## 2. Model Routing: The New Paradigm for Efficiency
A significant shift is underway towards “model routing,” an approach that optimizes AI usage by matching specific tasks to appropriate models. Hard problems are directed to top-tier, more powerful (and costly) models, while simpler or boilerplate tasks are handled by cheaper, faster, and “good enough” models. This method promises substantial cost efficiencies, with some seeing 5 to 10 times better cost efficiency, and is rapidly gaining adoption in the industry.

---

## 3. Cognition AI's Productivity Guarantee and ROI Measurement
Scott Wu from Cognition AI introduced their “AI Productivity Guarantee,” aiming to alleviate customer concerns about AI spending by ensuring measurable value. The guarantee focuses on output and productivity, rather than mere activity or token consumption. Cognition AI measures success by tracking hours of engineering effort saved and increased capacity, demonstrating concrete ROI, such as migrations forecasted for eight months being completed in eight days with AI assistance.

---

## 4. Cisco's Encounter with Tokenomics and Intelligent Routing
Jeetu Patel from Cisco highlights that even a massive tech company like Cisco overshot its AI budget significantly, despite pre-planning. This underscores the widespread nature of the “tokenomics” challenge. Cisco is actively developing and utilizing intelligent routing layers for its specialized cybersecurity and networking AI models. This internal adoption reflects the enterprise-wide recognition that intelligent model routing is crucial for cost control and efficiency.

---

## 5. The Rise of Desk-Side AI and Decentralized Computing
A new class of computing is emerging, bringing AI closer to the user through “desk-side computing” where local agents run on machines like Mac minis. This decentralizes AI processing, allowing substantial portions of workloads to be handled locally by smaller, more efficient models. While this reduces token costs, it necessitates a significant increase in network bandwidth, as agents generate 450% more network traffic than humans for the same tasks, creating a “networking supercycle.”

---

## 6. Trust, Security, and the Future of an Agentic AI Ecosystem
The conversation touches upon the critical aspects of trust and security in deploying AI agents. Enterprises are implementing human-in-the-loop checkpoints, allowing for gradual increases in autonomy as trust is built. The future of AI is envisioned as a “coordination of intelligence” with models deployed across phones, desk-side devices, the edge, and data centers, all working in concert. The growth of specialized, efficient models is fostering a diversified ecosystem, promising continued innovation and expanded AI adoption.

---

## Conclusion
The era of blindly using the most powerful AI model for every task is ending due to unsustainable costs. Model routing, combining frontier and cheaper models based on task complexity, is becoming essential for corporate AI strategies. Companies like Cognition AI and Cisco are at the forefront, developing solutions and guarantees to ensure measurable ROI and manage exploding tokenomics. This shift also heralds a decentralized future with desk-side AI and a need for robust, secure, and intelligently coordinated AI systems, ultimately expanding the overall AI market by making it more accessible and cost-effective.
