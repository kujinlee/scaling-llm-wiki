Early Attempts and Levinthal's Paradox (2:04–5:37) dig deeper ▶

Early experimental methods for determining protein structure were painstaking and expensive, highlighting the complexity described by Levinthal's paradox.

  • Historically, protein structures were determined using experimental methods like X-ray crystallography, a painstaking and expensive process.
  • John Kendrew spent 12 years to resolve myoglobin's structure, illustrating the extreme difficulty of the task.
  • The "protein folding problem" stems from Levinthal's paradox, which shows that a small protein can theoretically fold in an astronomical number of ways, making brute-force computation impossible.
