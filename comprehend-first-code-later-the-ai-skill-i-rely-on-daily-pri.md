## 1. The Shifting Role of a Software Engineer at Sentry
Priscilla, a Senior Software Engineer at Sentry, shares her radical shift from traditional coding to primarily 'prompting' since December 2023. She now manages a team of AI agents and even had her presentation generated by AI. This highlights Sentry's deep integration of AI across its operations, aiming to enhance productivity in their complex, 15-year-old codebase relied upon by 100,000 organizations.

---

## 2. Sentry's Internal AI Ecosystem and Codebase Challenges
Sentry, a full observability platform, has developed several internal AI tools to manage its vast codebase and workflows. These include `Abacus` for tracking AI usage, `Warden` as a code review agent for PRs, and `Junior`, a Slack bot that analyzes bug reports and automatically creates PRs for fixes. The codebase is a constantly moving target, with approximately 100 PRs merged daily, new components, deprecations, and lint rules, making continuous understanding a significant challenge for engineers.

----- 

## 3. Unlocking Productivity: AI for Comprehension, Not Just Generation
Priscilla's personal analysis of her AI usage revealed a surprising insight: 67% of her prompts were dedicated to *comprehension*, while only 2% involved code *generation*. This finding counters the common perception that AI's primary benefit for developers is writing code. Instead, AI significantly accelerates the understanding phase, allowing her to quickly grasp incident root causes or historical product decisions that previously required tedious manual investigation or waiting for colleague responses across time zones.

---

## 4. "Catch Me Up": A Custom AI Skill for Deep Code Exploration
To systematize her comprehension needs, Priscilla developed a personal AI "skill" called "catch me up." This detailed, locally stored prompt structures comprehension questions into six specific exploration modes: Architecture, Convention, Feature Trace, Syntax, Testing, and History. She demonstrated how this skill provides structured insights, like organograms or tables, to quickly onboard her to new repositories or gain sufficient context to confidently review a colleague's pull request.

---

## 5. The Imperative of Human Understanding in the AI Era
The speaker emphasizes that while AI can perform research and planning, engineers must still *understand* AI's outputs to steer it correctly and avoid "vibe coding" – shipping code without full comprehension. Referencing Jack Nation's and Armin Ronacher's concerns, she argues that the biggest unlock from AI in a large codebase is enhanced comprehension, which empowers developers to maintain a mental model of their code and ensure high-quality contributions ("keynote code") rather than "slop code."

---

## Conclusion
The presentation powerfully asserts that AI's most impactful role for software engineers in large, complex codebases is not in merely generating code, but in dramatically improving comprehension. By acting as an "always-on senior engineer" that never tires of questions, AI enables developers to align their mental models with the code, fostering deeper understanding and more informed decision-making. This approach ensures that even with advanced AI assistance, engineers remain deeply connected to and responsible for the quality of the software they deliver.
