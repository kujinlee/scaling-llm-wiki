## 1. The AI Power User Mindset
AI usage has evolved significantly since 2022. While AI novices might use models like a Google search for simple questions, AI power users leverage detailed context, ask complex questions, and provide ample background information (e.g., project data, voice memos) to enable comprehensive responses. This approach is akin to having empathy for the AI, ensuring it has enough information to perform well.

A key distinction lies in seeking honest feedback. Power users craft neutral questions or provide rubrics to prevent sycophancy (AI's tendency to agree) and ensure objective critiques. For writing tasks, instead of requesting direct text (which often results in "AI slop"), power users employ progressive outlining, iterating on outlines and bullet points before generating the final draft.

---

## 2. Understanding AI's Knowledge & Research Tools
AI models possess vast "pre-trained knowledge" acquired from reading trillions of words on the internet. This training enables them to answer common questions and even understand text with typos due to exposure to imperfect online data.

AI's ability to access real-time information relies on **web search**. This is triggered for queries about current events (e.g., a 2025 meme), location-specific details (e.g., gyms near a city), or niche topics not covered in its pre-trained data. Web search can be automatic or explicitly prompted. Users can also guide AI to prioritize reliable sources (e.g., official health organizations) to avoid misinformation from popular but less credible sites like social media.

For highly complex tasks requiring synthesis of many sources, **deep research** is utilized. This involves "agentic AI" that formulates a research plan, executes multiple parallel web searches, evaluates relevance, and synthesizes findings into a detailed report. Deep research is suited for tasks taking minutes or hours of human effort, like planning a Halloween haunted house, and is typically explicitly triggered by the user.

## 3. AI as a Collaborative Thought Partner
AI serves as an excellent thought partner for brainstorming and complex problem-solving. For effective **brainstorming**, users should provide extensive relevant context (e.g., personal details for a workout plan) and then iterate with the AI by requesting multiple options and providing specific feedback. This nudges the AI beyond common-sense, generic ideas towards more creative and personalized solutions.

Modern AI models demonstrate strong **reasoning capabilities**, capable of handling tasks that would take humans many hours. To leverage this, users can instruct the AI to "think hard" or "ultra-think" in their prompts. This prompts the AI to reason at length, potentially for several minutes, gathering additional information and refining its conclusions through multiple rounds before delivering a final answer. Users are encouraged to assign hard, real-world tasks to AI, providing all the necessary context a human expert would require for successful completion.

## 4. Crafting High-Quality Output: Writing, Editing & Avoiding Slop
AI-generated content often suffers from "AI slop" – text that appears well-written superficially but lacks substance, overuses specific phrases (e.g., "not X but Y," "delve," "nuanced"), and lacks unique insights. To avoid this, an effective strategy for **writing** with AI is **progressive outlining**. This involves first asking the AI to generate an outline, then iteratively refining it with feedback, expanding it into bullet points, and only then generating the final text. This high-leverage process allows for efficient revisions by changing small parts of the outline that impact large sections of the final article.

For **editing and critique**, working on an article piece by piece (sentence or paragraph at a time) is more manageable than large-scale edits. To receive objective, helpful feedback, it's crucial to provide AI with a clear, unambiguous **rubric** or grading criteria. These rubrics define specific, measurable standards (e.g., "Does every named character have a goal? Yes/No"), forcing the AI to evaluate work against a defined standard rather than giving sycophantic praise. While cross-model review (one AI critiquing another's output) can offer marginal benefits, routinely testing different AI models for specific tasks is more practical due to their "jagged intelligence" and rapid evolution.

## 5. Beyond Text: Multimodal AI & Application Development
AI's capabilities extend beyond text to **multimodal outputs** including images, videos, voices, and even code. Examples include generating unique cake designs, restoring old photos, creating short videos, cloning voices for audio content, and programming simple games (e.g., typing games, fireworks simulators) or utility apps (e.g., Pomodoro timers, bill calculators). The cost and time for generation vary significantly: text is most efficient, followed by speech, images, and then videos, which are the most expensive and slowest.

AI can also handle **multimodal inputs**, interpreting images (e.g., whiteboards, handwritten notes, receipts) to extract context, although it may struggle with fine-grain visual details. For **application development and data analysis**, AI can write and execute code. Users can upload spreadsheets of personal or business data (e.g., running logs, sales records) and prompt the AI to analyze trends, generate graphs, and extract insights without needing to write any code themselves. This "code execution tool" allows AI to perform calculations, plot data, and provide data-driven conclusions, making complex analysis accessible.

## Conclusion
Becoming an AI power user involves a deep understanding of how AI models work, their capabilities, and their limitations. By mastering techniques such as providing rich context, employing iterative feedback loops, understanding when to use different research tools, framing prompts neutrally, and leveraging multimodal functionalities, individuals can transform AI into an indispensable thought partner and tool for complex tasks, leading to significant personal and professional benefits. Continuous experimentation with new models and giving AI challenging problems will further hone these critical skills.
