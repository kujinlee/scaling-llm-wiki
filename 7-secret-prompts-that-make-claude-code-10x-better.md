## 1. Ultra Think for Maximum Reasoning
This secret code, `ultra think`, instructs Claude to apply its maximum reasoning effort for a specific prompt, regardless of your default settings. It's ideal for complex or technical tasks where deep thought is required, allowing you to save tokens by using lower reasoning efforts for simpler, everyday prompts. When activated, Claude prioritizes spending more time thinking and reasoning for that particular query.

---

## 2. {slash} caveman for Token Optimization
The `{slash} caveman` skill is an open-source tool designed to significantly reduce the number of output tokens generated by Claude. This is particularly beneficial for users on lower-tier plans or those needing to carefully manage their token usage. It can be easily installed by asking Claude to do so, and then invoked in prompts using the `/caveman` command.

---

## 3. {slash} insights for Personalized Productivity Analysis
The `{slash} insights` command allows Claude to analyze your past 30 days of usage. It generates a detailed HTML report highlighting your effective usage patterns, identifying anti-patterns, suggesting areas for improvement, and recommending specific commands, prompts, or features tailored to your actual workflow. This provides a personalized reality check on how well you're leveraging Claude and where you can gain more productivity, making it useful for a monthly review.

---

## 4. {slash} loop for Local Recurring Tasks
With `{slash} loop`, you can schedule recurring tasks for Claude directly on your computer. These tasks can repeat at intervals as short as one minute and last for up to three days. It's important to note that for `{slash} loop` tasks to run, your computer must remain on and your Claude session active, as they are dependent on your local environment.

---

## 5. {slash} schedule for Cloud-Based Automation
In contrast to `{slash} loop`, the `{slash} schedule` command enables recurring tasks to run independently in the cloud. This means your computer doesn't need to be on, making it suitable for daily stand-ups, recurring reports, or other automations that don't require local file access. Intervals are longer (minimum one hour), and there are daily run limits based on your Claude plan, but scheduled tasks do not expire in three days.

---

## 6. {slash} btw (by the way) for Parallel Inquiries
The `{slash} btw` or `by the way` command is extremely useful for maintaining workflow continuity. It allows you to ask Claude a secondary question or run a separate prompt while it's still busy with a main, long-running task. Claude will answer your `btw` query without interrupting its primary operation, preserving the context of your ongoing conversation and effectively allowing for parallel task management within the same session.

---

## 7. {slash} clear for Context Reset
The `{slash} clear` command resets the context of your current Claude session by deleting all previous conversation history. This is crucial for two main reasons: it helps prevent AI accuracy degradation that can occur with excessive context, and it saves tokens by ensuring Claude doesn't process irrelevant past information for new tasks. It's highly recommended to use `{slash} clear` when switching between distinct tasks to maintain optimal performance and manage token consumption.

## Conclusion
These seven secret codes offer powerful ways to significantly enhance your productivity and efficiency when using Claude AI. From maximizing reasoning and optimizing token usage to scheduling tasks and managing conversational context, leveraging these commands can unlock new levels of interaction and automation, allowing users to get much more out of their AI assistant than with basic prompting alone. Incorporating these into your workflow can save time, improve output quality, and better manage your resources within Claude.
